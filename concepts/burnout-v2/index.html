Abstract

v2 fixes three holes in v1: (i) the collapse threshold ρ* is individual, not universal — we replace ρ_burn = 1 with a population threshold distribution; (ii) 'sustained period' becomes a hazard rate B(t) with explicit timescales; (iii) the overhead/output split is observer-dependent because facade maintenance counts as productive output for the firm and as overhead for the person doing it. Under realistic parameters, a person at ρ^P = 1.50 (collapse-imminent in personal accounting) registers as ρ^F = 0.54 on the firm's dashboard — same hour of work, two valid bookkeepings, almost a full unit apart. The observer-relative split explains why firm-level metrics systematically underestimate burnout and why individual interventions outperform organizational ones in expectation.
