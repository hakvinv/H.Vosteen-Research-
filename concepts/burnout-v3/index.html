Abstract

v3 — the new-job decay edition. v2 explained why rest fails; it did not explain why a brand-new job feels different at month six. Same hours, same desk, same colleagues, less will to start the day. The numerator did not change — the denominator did. v3 adds the missing term: hedonic decay of perceived output value. We introduce a multiplier m(t) ∈ (0, 1] that decays from 1 toward a job-match floor m_∞, and write ρ^P(t) = ρ_0 / m(t). Under realistic parameters, a new job at ρ_0 = 0.6 (well in the safe zone) drifts to ρ^P = 1.5 over twelve months purely because the pride-and-novelty multiplier collapses from 1 to 0.4 — no extra meetings, no extra interrupts, the same hour of work just stops counting for as much. The decay rate τ_m and floor m_∞ are individually measurable and predict the burnout-onset distribution.
