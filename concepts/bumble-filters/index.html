Abstract

Dating-app filters collapse the pool multiplicatively, not additively. For n independent filters with average pass-rate p̄, surviving pool size is N(n) = N_0 · p̄^n. Two typical filters (distance 0.85, age 0.70) eliminate ~40% of the pool; six eliminate 95%+; ten approach statistical zero. This is the curse of dimensionality for compatibility: log N(n) = log N_0 + n · log p̄, linear in filter count with negative slope. Human probability intuition is approximately additive for small n, which is why the collapse feels counterintuitive. The optimal filter count is small (2–4); beyond that, lost pool size outweighs additional selectivity and the 'perfect partner' filter becomes an empty set.
