Abstract

ADHD is usually framed as a deficit. Under a dynamical-systems reading, attention is a state in a phase space with multiple stable attractors. Neurotypical brains occupy a basin near the profile Φ = T − R ≈ 0 (balanced transformation rate and filter friction). ADHD brains occupy a distinct basin near Φ ≈ +2.5 with high variance in attentional bandwidth. Under a two-component free-energy generative model (constraint vs. novelty), the steady-state distribution of Φ is bimodal with an unstable saddle between basins. Stimulants shift the attractor geometrically toward the NT basin rather than eliminating any deficit. Bridge equation: Φ = π_T^-1 − π_R^-1. Testable predictions include failure of Hartigan's dip test for unimodality and reaction-time-variance AUC > 0.85 for ADHD vs non-ADHD separation.
