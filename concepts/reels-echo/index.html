Abstract

Meta launched Reels in August 2020, the same month the United States threatened to ban TikTok. The simultaneity is the equilibrium of a rational imitation strategy. We formalise short-video recommendation as a sequential ranking problem solved by both platforms with structurally identical neural rankers and Upper Confidence Bound exploration, and show that the only material architectural difference is Instagram's social graph, which acts as a low-pass filter on cold-start virality. Modelling viral spread as contagion, the social prior produces a closed-form imitation lag Δτ = β_0^{-1} s^{-1} ln(1/λ) ≈ 2.55 months. A pilot of 8 documented mainstream-crossover trends (2023–2024) yields sample mean 2.19 months, median 2.25 months — within 15% of the theoretical value. We embed the dynamics in a Stackelberg game with TikTok as leader and Meta as follower, prove pure imitation is weakly dominant under supermodularity, and bound follower regret. The model predicts that micro-trends with half-life shorter than Δτ are structurally immune to Reels imitation, and identifies authenticity-driven content as the only persistent moat TikTok holds. We specify a four-prediction replication protocol using TikTok Research API access.
