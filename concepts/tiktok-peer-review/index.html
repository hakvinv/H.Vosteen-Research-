Abstract

Academic peer review is sequential. Platform comments are parallel. The latency ratio τ_a/τ_p ≈ 3,000 produces a 9,000× speedup at typical parameters. The depth condition requires N_p ≥ 71 substantive commenters when q_a = 0.7 and q_p = 0.05. We compute both quantities and identify the regime in which platform comments substitute for academic peer review. The model predicts cycle times of two to three weeks for early-stage iteration, consistent with the dz-cascade on B1 Burnout (TikTok, April 2026), where a substantive flaw was identified within 24 hours of posting and the model revised within 14 days. The equivalence breaks for replication, longitudinal validation, and any task requiring institutional infrastructure.
