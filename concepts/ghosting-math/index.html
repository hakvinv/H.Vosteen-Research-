Abstract

Ghosting is usually framed as cowardice. Under a sequential dyadic exit game, the equilibrium is determined by the Silence-Cost-Ratio σ_R = c_silence / c_response. When σ_R > 1 the subgame-perfect equilibrium is honest exit; when σ_R < 1 it is ghosting; the transition is sharp. σ_R is driven by the recurrence probability p that the parties or their networks meet again. Pre-2010 (small networks, high p): σ_R ≈ 3. On dating apps (p → 0): σ_R ≈ 0. The shift is structural, not generational — replacing the user pool with people from 1995 under the same platform architecture produces the same equilibrium. The fix is mechanism redesign (e.g. Hinge's 'Your Turn' adds algorithmic c_silence) rather than individual moral change.
