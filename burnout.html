Each is the live form of a figure from Burnout Is Not Too Much Work. Math is in the panel headings, controls are the parameters, the plot is the answer.

1. Observer-relative split

ρᴾ = a / b    ρᶠ = (a − f) / (b + f)    gap = f(a+b) / (b(b+f))

Same hour of work, two valid bookkeepings. a is overhead the person counts, b is real output the person values, f is the facade portion of a the firm folds into output. The wedge is the gap between the two ratios.

2. Drift dynamic

dρ/dt = α − β·r·ρ  ⇒  ρ_ss = α / (β·r),   τ = 1 / (β·r)

Friction drifts under environmental injection α, drained by recovery intensity r scaled by per-person sensitivity β. The trajectory relaxes to ρ_ss with timescale τ. Burnout is ρ_ss > ρ*. Try lowering r without changing α to see why a sabbatical alone does not fix burnout.

3. Population sigmoid B(t)

B(t) = Φ( (μ₀ + δ·t − μ*) / √(σ² + σ*²) )

Population fraction in burnout under heterogeneous drift (within-person spread σ) and heterogeneous threshold (between-person spread σ*). δ is cohort-mean log-ρ drift per week, μ* is the median log-threshold. The dot is the chosen observation time t.
